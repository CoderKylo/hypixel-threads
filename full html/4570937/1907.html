What's up? I'm Siftz, and I will be teaching you how to protect any account. Wether if it's a Google account, Minecraft account, or any account, this guide will defenitely help you increase the security of your account/s, and will also decrease the chances of your account/s getting hacked or stolen. I hope this thread helps you even just a little bit. Enjoy!

Why Do I Need To Protect My Account?
Well, that's a simple question. You need to protect your account because there are personal information inside it. If your account gets stolen, a person (or even people) you don't know will be able to see your personal information. The attacker or hacker MIGHT be able to know your phone number, email, and other personal details. So yeah, you need to protect your account for obvious reasons.

Step 1: Make Sure Your Account Has A Strong Password
Your account should have a strong password. If you feel like your password is weak, and easy to guess, then that means that your account is in trouble. If you feel that you need to change your password to something stronger, then go ahead. This first step is already a HUGE thing in protecting your account. Make sure the new password is long by the way. A strong password is always a long password. Also, don't make it obvious lol.

Step 2: Don't Share Your Account Details
NEVER share your account details. The account details include the password, email, phone number (if it has one), etc. The only people you can really trust are your parents or guardians. Don't tell your friends or anyone you meet. Yes, this includes your crush

Step 3: Be Safe When Recording Or Streaming
Hey Siftz, what do you mean by this? What I mean by this is; when you're recording or streaming, you should ALWAYS make sure that you aren't exposing any sensitive information. There are people who have sadly lost their accounts with this. Make sure that your viewers are not seeing any account information. Some people forget that they are recording or streaming that they make the mistake of looking at their account in the middle of filming a video or stream.

Thanks for taking your time reading this! If you think that this thread is missing something, then please simply reply to this thread. I will try my best to read all the replies. Once you are done with this thread, your account should be safe from hackers. You no longer have to worry about your account getting hacked or stolen either. Again, I hope this thread helped you even just a little bit

I mean, there's a lot missing from this.
Passwords only follow one of the 3 methods of MFA.
Out of all 3, a password is only considered as something you know. For ideal account security, you'll want to mention the other 2 factors, something you are, and something you have.

For example, something you are could be an authentication app. Although neither Microsoft nor Mojang have this feature, you can use it on an external device such as a phone with both touch and face ID.

Something you have is the most secure of all the methods of account security. Off of Amazon, you can buy a multi-factor authentication drive for 15 bucks. This singular tool requires you to insert a physical drive into your device before you can log into anything. Considering Minecraft is something most people just do in their own homes, this will be even more secure as it's likely never going to leave. Authentication drives for things such as phones are slightly worse, as you need to physically bring it around with you everywhere, if you lose it then 1. Logging into stuff will be hell. 2. Someone out there has the potential to log into your account.

Although, for Microsoft they have 2 methods of the "Something you know" authentication. Passwords and 2Fa. Regarding the latter, OP didn't even mention it. I somewhat understand why, it could cause some confusion by the near majority of people who haven't migrated their account yet, but having that knowledge beforehand is always good, especially since people's Microsoft accounts are statistically weaker by their own doing than Mojang accounts.

Exposing your data during a stream is already really, really hard to do. Not only are passwords asterisked, but most software also blackout the screen if you swap to another tab.
