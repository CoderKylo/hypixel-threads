For me, the Middle School and High School are literally on the same campus within a minute's walk from each other. Now, I went from being homeschooled to going to High School this year so I can't comment, but it's basically the same exact building on the same exact bus, just a few hours earlier, so I wouldn't say there's a huge shift.

Culturally though? The difference is huge. There's a lot less High Schools than there are Elementary/Middle Schools (there's something like 5 elementary schools in my town but only 1 high school), so you're going to know a lot less people. You're also changing classes which means you don't spend all day around the same 20-ish kids. Also, teens are just a lot different than little kids (you're going to find a lot more people wanting to get laid)

Overall, travel doesn't really matter. Public Schools will give you dedicated buses (U.S cities were designed for cars, European cities weren't) and if you're in one town the middle/high schools won't be too far apart. The biggest difference is going from what seems to be a little kid to a big kid in a year or two, especially with COVID when you go from 8th grade to 10th grade in the blink of an eye.
