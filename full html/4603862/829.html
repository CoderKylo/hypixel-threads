So this may not come as a surprise to few but as time goes on the pvp client scene as a whole has had me pulling out more hair then ever. This is for many reasons which stretch from a lack of creativity, to the user base to how clients are rated. I personally don't really have any bias towards any client, I did use lunar but rarely play now and instead use forge however I won't really be adding that to the comparison here as this is mainly the pvp client scene, not really the modding scene. That aside, I haven't made a long thread in a while so here's what I got to say.

Creativity:

My first issue with pvp clients is the sheer lack of creativity many of them have. They quite literally all look the same and don't strive to try new things and make something more unique than the other clients. Here is Blc vs Lunar:

Blc:


Lunar:


They literally look the same, just different subheadings. And sure badlion could have had heavy inspiration but if they did why are they so damn similar, this really shows a lack of innovation or willingness to try something new or even compete with eachother.

Now let's look at main menus:
Blc:


Lunar:


At this point they are quite literally the same thing. EVEN THE STORE IS IN THE SAME PLACE?!?!?! And sure this main menu has the classic minecraft layout and isn't the worst, it's just that it's been done the same way in every single client and even in the game. There could be at least some attempt to spice things up.

Another aspect of creativity is the mods they come pre-included with, sure lunar advertises 65+ mods and badlion advertises 50+ mods but how many of these mods actually server a purpose other than to fill your screen with rgb. The only useful mods badlion has are 1.7 animations, armor status, autogg, autotip, coordinates, direction, full bright, item counter, potion status, replay mod and lastly toggle sprint. That's only 11 mods from 50+. Lunar has similar stuff and advertises more mods so I won't bother listing them all but you should get the idea of what's considered something that's useful. You could also add in clear glass, clear water, item physics and skyblock addons but these are more cosmetic based that may or may not impact gameplay.

So out of all those mods, guess how many are unique from the different clients? NONE
Ik shocker, both clients have similar or identical mods and not much else in between. And while these mods are good, there is no creativity being put towards creating new mods, just the same thing. It's just the same things, nothing new.

This causes actual mod advantage of the client to be an almost non existent talking point.

User Base:

Now onto the user base, the userbase of the clients is fine and to be expected, it's just how they talk about the clients that's really really annoying. They keep talking about one client being "clean", or "giving more fps" or just saying they feel better playing with it. People saying a client is clean is annoying because all they assume clean means is how the client looks aesthetically, and while this is a talking point the biggest thing they miss is the actual functionality of the ui. I can make a simple clean main menu that has only 1 button and when pressed gives you a maze of buttons to just open 1 thing. This is obviously not practical but somehow if promoted right would make everyone think it's revolutionary and even more clean than another client. Imo functionality should be more important than how it looks like. Why have a super simplistic design if it takes you forever to get to something.

Client Rating:

Onto the next talking point is one client "giving more fps" than another. And while fps is a quantitative value that can be compared, what can't be compared is how you test it. You can't just go into an empty lobby and say this fps is better compared to testing a client in a full lobby and complaining about stuttering. And even then, most people just eyeball it which introduces the issue of personal bias thus making the results skewed and not accurate.

To top this issue off another annoyance is how those clients gain that extra fps and how people actually claim it to be. Pretty much most clients use optifine as their basis for performance and just modify it to their liking. However what this means is that most of the optimization is done by optifine and not the actual client. So then what's the point in saying this client is better than that one if they are all based on the same optimization mod. Sure their fps might vary but it may not be too significant. Another issue with comparing fps is that different devices simply function differently, so there is no definitive answer. While one client may improve fps, there is no way of telling how well another client will do without trying it.

Final Thoughts:

I wont bore you anymore but if you add in the fact how closed source these clients are, badlion requiring an anti-cheat and the cosmetic system, it really does raise questions on how good are these clients. I don't want to say the developers didn't do anything, it's just that when comparing clients to clients, there appears to be very little difference. And these clients then inspire people to create copy cats which make the whole situation worse imo.

I hate to say it but I believe that hacked clients, yes hacked clients are more creative than pvp clients right now and are leading the way and pvp clients are just copying them. And I think it's been like this for a few years now even.

As a veteran of minecraft and the hypixel server I only really see these things getting worse and as more and more new generations of players start joining they'll have the mindset that this is the best the community has to offer.

I'll see where I can take this but that's it for now, let me know what you think.

AlphaCloud said:
So this may not come as a surprise to few but as time goes on the pvp client scene as a whole has had me pulling out more hair then ever. This is for many reasons which stretch from a lack of creativity, to the user base to how clients are rated. I personally don't really have any bias towards any client, I did use lunar but rarely play now and instead use forge however I won't really be adding that to the comparison here as this is mainly the pvp client scene, not really the modding scene. That aside, I haven't made a long thread in a while so here's what I got to say.

Creativity:

My first issue with pvp clients is the sheer lack of creativity many of them have. They quite literally all look the same and don't strive to try new things and make something more unique than the other clients. Here is Blc vs Lunar:

Blc:
View attachment 2773996

Lunar:
View attachment 2773999

They literally look the same, just different subheadings. And sure badlion could have had heavy inspiration but if they did why are they so damn similar, this really shows a lack of innovation or willingness to try something new or even compete with eachother.

Now let's look at main menus:
Blc:
View attachment 2774000

Lunar:
View attachment 2774001

At this point they are quite literally the same thing. EVEN THE STORE IS IN THE SAME PLACE?!?!?! And sure this main menu has the classic minecraft layout and isn't the worst, it's just that it's been done the same way in every single client and even in the game. There could be at least some attempt to spice things up.

Another aspect of creativity is the mods they come pre-included with, sure lunar advertises 65+ mods and badlion advertises 50+ mods but how many of these mods actually server a purpose other than to fill your screen with rgb. The only useful mods badlion has are 1.7 animations, armor status, autogg, autotip, coordinates, direction, full bright, item counter, potion status, replay mod and lastly toggle sprint. That's only 11 mods from 50+. Lunar has similar stuff and advertises more mods so I won't bother listing them all but you should get the idea of what's considered something that's useful. You could also add in clear glass, clear water, item physics and skyblock addons but these are more cosmetic based that may or may not impact gameplay.

So out of all those mods, guess how many are unique from the different clients? NONE
Ik shocker, both clients have similar or identical mods and not much else in between. And while these mods are good, there is no creativity being put towards creating new mods, just the same thing. It's just the same things, nothing new.

This causes actual mod advantage of the client to be an almost non existent talking point.

User Base:

Now onto the user base, the userbase of the clients is fine and to be expected, it's just how they talk about the clients that's really really annoying. They keep talking about one client being "clean", or "giving more fps" or just saying they feel better playing with it. People saying a client is clean is annoying because all they assume clean means is how the client looks aesthetically, and while this is a talking point the biggest thing they miss is the actual functionality of the ui. I can make a simple clean main menu that has only 1 button and when pressed gives you a maze of buttons to just open 1 thing. This is obviously not practical but somehow if promoted right would make everyone think it's revolutionary and even more clean than another client. Imo functionality should be more important than how it looks like. Why have a super simplistic design if it takes you forever to get to something.

Client Rating:

Onto the next talking point is one client "giving more fps" than another. And while fps is a quantitative value that can be compared, what can't be compared is how you test it. You can't just go into an empty lobby and say this fps is better compared to testing a client in a full lobby and complaining about stuttering. And even then, most people just eyeball it which introduces the issue of personal bias thus making the results skewed and not accurate.

To top this issue off another annoyance is how those clients gain that extra fps and how people actually claim it to be. Pretty much most clients use optifine as their basis for performance and just modify it to their liking. However what this means is that most of the optimization is done by optifine and not the actual client. So then what's the point in saying this client is better than that one if they are all based on the same optimization mod. Sure their fps might vary but it may not be too significant. Another issue with comparing fps is that different devices simply function differently, so there is no definitive answer. While one client may improve fps, there is no way of telling how well another client will do without trying it.

Final Thoughts:

I wont bore you anymore but if you add in the fact how closed source these clients are, badlion requiring an anti-cheat and the cosmetic system, it really does raise questions on how good are these clients. I don't want to say the developers didn't do anything, it's just that when comparing clients to clients, there appears to be very little difference. And these clients then inspire people to create copy cats which make the whole situation worse imo.

I hate to say it but I believe that hacked clients, yes hacked clients are more creative than pvp clients right now and are leading the way and pvp clients are just copying them. And I think it's been like this for a few years now even.

As a veteran of minecraft and the hypixel server I only really see these things getting worse and as more and more new generations of players start joining they'll have the mindset that this is the best the community has to offer.

I'll see where I can take this but that's it for now, let me know what you think.
