Okay here's some actual advice coming from someone who can jitter (13cps) and butterfly (22cps):

I have been doing both for awhile now and can say that the biggest thing you need is practice. If you want to learn to jitter, it will take a long time for you to get high cps with good aim. If you are new to pvp and want to quickly learn the basics, i recommend butterfly or normal click until you understand movement and have good aim, then you can focus on jittering. However if you already have good w-taps and mechanics, or don't mind spending many months to master jittering, here is my advice:

Without holding your mouse, make a muscle with your right arm as if you are doing a bicep curl. Then, while keeping the tension, straighten your arm out and continue to flex your arm. YOU MAY NOT GET IT RIGHT AWAY!! That's the biggest thing with jittering-- it takes time. But if you are really still not having any results, you may need to focus on strengthening your arms.

I just want to conclude by saying that I think you should eventually learn to jitter, but it may not be best for you right now.

TLDR: Normal/butterfly if you want to learn quickly. If you want to jitter it will take a lot of practice, but is worth it.

I've been jitter clicking for about 2 years now, so I'd like to put some input into this topic.

Jittering is really hard at first, and yes it hurts to do when you first start out, but over time if you keep doing it, the difficulty goes away, and while you will strain your arm if you do it too long, there isn't any major pain from it (you'll strain your arm/wrist from basically any clicking method doing it for hours on end). Here's some tips I have to get better!

- Find a grip that suits you. Because jittering involves vibrating your whole arm, you're free to use any style of grip that makes your arm most comfortable.

- Comfort is key. You have to reduce the stress on your arm as much as possible, so that you can jitter either a higher cps, or for a longer duration. I would advise going for a grip that's more comfortable than for one that you maybe get higher cps with.

- Practice! Rome wasn't built in a day, and neither will your jitter clicking skill. Just jitter clicking sometimes while scrolling some website or watching a video will build up over time. To practice aim, I'd suggest setting your sensitivity to hyper speed, then trying to jitter. Aim for the least amount of shake as possible. Lastly, and most obviously, practice in coordination with movement (basically just pvp). Once you're comfortable enough jittering, try to get used to block-hitting and movement while jittering.

- Lastly, and I know this is corny but, exercise! Seriously, I'm telling you, I used to average 10-11 jitter but as soon as I started working out sometime last spring, I've been averaging 13-14. (Also, if you exercise you'll just be happier overall and it may lead to a better playing experience, idk).

That's all I have, but I hoped I helped somewhat!

I've been jitter clicking for around 9 months now and I have some tips:
- Before you start jitter clicking, do something to warm up your clicking hand and your arm. I usually do 8-10 pushups before jittering, and if my hands are really cold (I live in Canada brrr) I run my hands under warm water or something
- I like to jitter click by tensing my arm so my brachioradialis muscle (I used a muscle diagram ok) is pretty tense and do my wrist is slightly locked
- Basically when I jitter click I use the tension in my arm to sorta vibrate it (I practiced before on tables and just on mice, holding the mouse with my left hand so it wouldn't shake and jittering with my right)
- Once I got the hang of jittering, I had to focus on aiming, so at first I just walked up to people ingame and started jittering at them (and I usually died), but my aim started getting better. This is sorta boring if you're not getting any progress, so I joined the Pit and did this in mid, and it actually really helped
- I decided to use my index and middle finger to jitter because it gives me more stamina and cps, personally
- I now use a control mousepad and turned down my sensitivity a bit so I can aim better

Now I can jitter consistently at 15 cps
