This thread is not about any specific case of harassment, abuse, or bullying. This is also a very serious topic. Please do not bring up specific cases unless you are using them as an example.

Also just in case this gets deleted for any reason, I have it saved in multiple places.


Many people who share their experience of online bullying, harassment, or even... inappropriate kinds of abuse. Most people will often say the same thing along the lines of "you could have just blocked them" or "just block them," but that is super flawed and may cause more problems than it solves.

Firstly, it may make the victim feel stupid, causing them to have thoughts such as "why didn't i just block them" or "i could have just blocked them" which may cause them to feel bad about themself, like making them think they didn't block said person because they're too stupid to realize what was actually happen. Instead of saying "just block them," you could say something more supportive such as "im sorry, i'm sure you'll recover" which doesn't make the victim feel stupid.

A lot of times, people will talk in certain ways to try and manipulate the victim into believing what they're doing is fine, or they may actually think what they're doing is fine. No matter what, most the time the victims usually will end up blocking, however that isn't always the case. No matter what the reason for not blocking is, victims shouldn't feel bad about not blocking.

There are many reasons why victims may not block said abuser. They may be manipulated into not doing it, they may not be doing well mentally, maybe they even think it's normal (literally just paraphrased what @Pugmints told me). Whatever it is, most likely the person saying "just block them" wouldn't block them if they were in the same situation.

idk how to end this so yeah tl;dr below


TL;DR: Don't say "should have just blocked them" to any victims of online abuse, harassment, or bullying.

this wasn’t online, but a few years ago, i was with my mom and sister at the store. she said she was going to go get something from another aisle. she told me this a few months after it happened, but when she went to go get that something, a man got in her way and tried to lure her outside. she didn’t know how to react to a situation beyond random and unprecedented even knowing what to do. she stood there stunned as the man kept going on and on about her looks. this went for around 15 seconds or so until i got bored and started wandering around aisles until i saw my sister running back to the the cart past the man, telling me “lets go cmon.” i looked behind me to see a man staring in my direction and got kinda spooked so we hurried out.

as the fool i was, i didn’t see into what happened in those 15 seconds.

after a month or so when we were eating lunch together, she told me this story along with what her friend had to say about it. her friend said something along the lines of, “should have reacted better” or “should’ve known better.”

well, you know what. what would you have done in that situation? how would you have reacted? would you like it if someone said “shouldn’t have gotten involved” right in your face when you don’t know what the outcome of the situation you are in could be?

my sister felt almost as if it was her fault for not knowing better. as if it was her fault the 40 year old man came up to her. as if it was her fault she decided to get something from another aisle. if anything, the only person to blame is the person coming up to you in the first place. not yourself or your reactions.

that form of manipulation is so vicious and evil. from a situation that could have ended up on the news, telling someone those words feel almost as if an insult. i can’t even describe how it would be with words because of how terrible it is.

good point to bring across though. i see this happen really often and not many people actually mention it.
