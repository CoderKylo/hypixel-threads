Rugs​

i’m basically paraphrasing to the point where it looks like plagiarism, but a little on the edge so who really knows

The first ever rugs were made by nomadic people in central Asia thousands of years ago. Since sheep were a thing, they used them to make wool, to make rugs. Rugs served as being warm during the cold winters.

rugs could be used as the follwing:

• tent flaps
• wall tapestries
• table/bench coverings
• saddlebags

Soon enough, rugs made their way into Europe by trade between both, them and Asia. Rich classes in Europe took a liking for the rugs.

Fast forward a bit, and boom, rugs in the US. This gave Americans something better to rely on rather than sticks and stuff basically like that.

In 1838, Erastus Bigelow then invented a much quicker way of weaving rugs, or a power loom I should say.

In the 1940’s and ‘50s, rugs become more popular with much better material.

I got a rug where I am rn and it has a fish on it which is really cool.

~~~~~
​
1. In the late 1800s, the city of Dalton, Georgia became home to a large portion of the carpet industry. It’s still currently referred to as the “Carpet Capital of the World.”

2. Prior to the 1950s, wool and cotton were the primary materials used in rugs. In the mid-50s synthetic materials such as nylon, polyester, polypropylene, and viscose began to be introduced.

3. Many manufacturers pair up with guest designers for exclusive collections, like Barclay Butera, Martha Stewart, and Isaac Mizrahi.

4. The phrase “to sweep something under the rug” originated in 1963.

5. Hand-woven rugs are flat rugs that do not have a pile. They are woven on looms out of a variety of materials and often have quite meticulous patterns. While one of the oldest types of rugs, it is enjoying a resurgence in contemporary fashion due to the demand for flatweaves and sarouks in layering designs.
