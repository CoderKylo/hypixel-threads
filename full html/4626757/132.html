Yo, I am so unsure what keyboard to buy I have some ideas but, I would like some other suggestions too. The top three keyboards I was looking into were Steelseries Apex 7, Ducky Keyboards, and Razer Blackwidow v2/v3. My budget is up to/around $130 USD. Size board TKL or full size. Playing games mostly Minecraft and CS: GO. I would really like other suggestions too as well as your insights.

Apex 7 TKL
Pros: Long-lasting, High tech/Design, Scroll for many controls, Very comfortable, variety of switches (red, brown, blue), changeable keycaps, and great RGB and software.
Cons: Keycaps aren't the best, (Changeable), high pricing, better options for the price (lower or same)


Ducky Keebs TKL
Pros: One step away from custom, great durability, a lot of variety in size, switch, design, comfortable for the most part, changeable switches and caps.
Cons: Some stabilizers SUCK


Razer Blackwidow V2/V3 TKL or FUll
Pros: Comfortable, high tech, great for gaming.
Cons: Better options, Pricey, no customization.

My top 2 picks would be the logitech g pro or the corsair k95 platinum as these are pretty much both of the best keyboards for both form factors. I personally have a corsair k68rgb and it's fine but not the choice if you don't like a hard wrist wrest. I prefer a hard plastic compared to a soft plushy one. However I'd recommend looking through all of them to see what keyboard best achieves what you want for the smallest price while also not missing out on features.

However some things I've noticed in the community regarding this stuff is people also care way to much on how keyboards sound, and way to much on customization. With sound you'll have your headphones on most of the time anyway, and if you're in calls or have people near you then going with a quieter switch like reds is the simple answer to this. As for customization, it tends to be a gimmick that most people rarely use/take advantage of or if they do has very little benefit.
