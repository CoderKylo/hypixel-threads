Quantum Mechanics for Dummies! ft Wallress!​

Alright, get out your notebooks class. You’re about to learn some quantum mechanics! The only reason why I’m doing this is because I actually find it really interesting; otherwise, I would have chosen pandas. By the way, I haven’t started this yet, but I know it’s going to take a lot of time so please consider reading it, thanks.

edit: 1 hour 29 mins

What is it?

- Quantum mechanics studies the smallest stuff in the universe. The “smallest stuff” is basically atoms which are made up from protons, neutrons, and electrons.

Have you ever been out on a nice sunny day? Just imagine the sky turning negative every now and then and the sun glitching out like a video game. That’s basically quantum mechanics. The study of the most bizarre topic known to this universe.

As the reader, you are probably both confused and intrigued by it.

The theory works!

- Quantum mechanics is one of the strongest scientific theories in literal history. As we go into studying the smallest particles in the universe, things aren’t like what you think they are. What goes on there is the bottom of the universe iceberg theory. Things that can’t be explained properly. Things that are just so weird an 8 year old would laugh at the topic.

History

The history begins with Max Planck, then to Albert Einstien, and then over to Niels Bohr, and now to people like Schrödinger and Heisenberg. Do those names sound familiar? I bet they do. The history will help us understand why the universe is so strange.

Max Planck and Black Body Radiation

Have you ever seen blue fire? Like on candles or birthday cakes? If so, it’s because of something called Black Body Radiation. Based on physics, colors are caused by different wavelengths of light.

- wavelength: distance between two peaks of a wave
- wave: something to do with light and frequency

Black body radiation describes how something can change color as it gets hot. When things get hot, it can change in the pattern shown below:

- red
- orange
- white
- blue

This seems similar to stars.

According to the law of conservation of energy, people thought that the hotter something gets, the brighter it gets. So shouldn’t blue fire turn ultraviolet?

LCE: energy cannot be created nor destroyed, but may be changed from one form to another

The Ultraviolet Catastrophe

Fun fact: the wavelengths reach a massive decline after reaching the blue spectrum. This is also known as the Ultraviolet Catastrophe (woah!!!!! how edgy!!!!).

Now things will get confusing for both me and you. Max Planck could only look at this in one way. He hypothesized that the energy jumps from one discrete wavelength to the next.

For example: the energy moves from position 1 to position 2 without going through position 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, etc. But this makes no sense. How does it do that? That’s ju- it- it makes literally no sense?

Planck’s Constant: Energy Jumps from Place to Place

Planck was an egotistical man. He made his own number!

Planck identified a number called “Planck’s Constant.” So, whenever the energy does that jump thing, it does so in multiples of Planck’s number. This number is incredibly minuscule. This means there is nothing between those leaps. It goes from 1x Planck’s Constant to 2x Planck’s Constant with nothing but literal empty space between the jumps.

It took a while before scientists realized just how ****ed up quantum mechanics really is.

Albert Einstein

You know the theory of relativity? Well who cares. Albert is known for his Photoelectric Effect that won him the Nobel Prize in physics. This has to do with the properties of light (sick background info!!!!).

Maxwell’s Light Experiment: how light moves like a wave

This is where things start to get interesting.

Imagine a pond. You toss two rocks into it. The ripples from both rocks take effect. Both ripples come together and cancel them out. Maxwell was able to prove that light worked the exact same.

Light is both Waves and Particles?

Albert had realized that light indeed is made up of teeny wittle particles. These particles behave like waves.

Light Photons as waves

Imagine a cardboard box with a light source around it. There’s a little hole in the box and have a sheet of paper which detects the light. Looking at the inside, you can see how light behaves like a wave.

This part is confusing.

What if we made the light extremely dim? Instead of a light source, how about a photon?

- photon: a light particle

Shouldn’t it do the same as the light source did?

Well no. The photon actually just flies in a random direction each time. This makes Newton sound like an idiot. It completely defies his law of motion. However, if you let one photon in at a time and keep it coming, eventually, the photons would start to depict the pattern of a wave.

Planck’s Constant gets stalked by Photons

Photons always fall into discrete units of energy. Wanna guess which one? No, you’re wrong, it’s Planck’s Constant. Einstein had concluded that each photon behaves like a part of a wave. Basically as if each water droplet in a wave was actually the wave itself.

The New Theory of Quantum Mechanics

The new theory makes things a whole ton weirder and just overall like the dab. You look at it in just complete utter shock.

It combines the ideas of Einstein and Bohr. instead of saying the elctrons orbit at an exact position, jumping from one to another, the new theory says their movements are even crazier.

Every scientist had different theories, but it came to the moment where they were all right with different answers. They just did the math differently. This basically means that at the bottom of everything, matter moves unpredictably. The tiny bits of matter soon form a wave like pattern, but if you just look at a single “quanta” (single electron or proton), their movements are just absurd.

An Incomplete Universe

Lets think of quantum mechanics as a video game.

You are in a small town and you see some houses. You can’t actually enter those houses because you aren’t supposed to. The programmers don’t want you to. But, you find a glitch that lets you enter it. When you enter it, there’s nothing. The programmers didn’t think you would enter it so there is just nothing.

This means we are allowed to enter, but it just wasn’t programmed. If the universe were a video game, quantum mechanics would be that glitch. We can look at what is going on at the smallest levels, but, (like the empty house) what we find there doesn’t make any sense.

“It's not that we can't make sense of quantum mechanics; it's that quantum mechanics shows that the universe doesn't make sense!”

“It's not that we can't make sense of quantum mechanics; it's that quantum mechanics shows that the universe doesn't make sense!”
