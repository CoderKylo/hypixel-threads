Ok so here's what I got:
https://de.pcpartpicker.com/list/NsfMLP
With this added too:

DEEPCOOL FANS HUB FH-10 DP-F10PWM HUB: Amazon.de: Computer & Accessories

DEEPCOOL FANS HUB FH-10 DP-F10PWM HUB: Amazon.de: Computer & Accessories
www.amazon.de

So you might notice that one very important part is missing which is the gpu. That's mostly cause imo on this tight budget and especially the current timing on getting a gpu for a reasonable price and then having it fit in this budget is not really possible nor worth it.

To even fit a gpu in this list you'd have to downgrade to a ryzen 3 or i3 which is doable but it's something not worth doing in the grand scheme of things. Even considering getting a ryzen 3 / i3 and using that extra money for a gpu, the gpu would already be quite dated(something like a gtx 1050) and might not even worth the extra money. It's better to live with integrated graphics for a year or two and then make a nice and complete purchase on a gpu. After all we are talking about mc so integrated graphics could definitely get the job done same with valorant.

If you or anyone else is curious, these were the cpu's I was comparing against:

minecraft is single threaded so getting a good single threaded cpu is what makes the difference. Also considering the integrated graphics included in the price and it makes sense going intel this time around. In the parts list I went with a 10600k as it's quite cheap but you still have to add the external cooler. The 11600k goes over budget a little and when comparing the performance gap from the 11600k to the 10600k the difference is minimal to almost non existent so it's not really worth it. The 3300x is also MORE than the 10600k so it doesn't even make sense going with it. Going with an i3 only leaves you room to get a gt 1030 which is pointless because the card it self was only designed to display output, nothing else.

As well nvidia nor amd have released any budget friendly desktop gpu's so far and rn depending on how things go they might not even do that and just jump ship straight to the 4000 series.
