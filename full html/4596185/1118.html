So, it’s 2021. The pandemic is still going on, although things are going back to normal, but still you feel the effects of the lockdown upon you. Typing on your old Thinkpad, trying to escape finishing that algebra assignment you sit and ponder about what it would be like to live a normal life and hang out with all of your friends. Well, you can hang out by using communication apps like Discord, and playing video games. There’s just one problem. Hardware. Your friends urge you to buy a PC, and so you check your local SuperBuy. The more powerful systems use AMD Ryzen chips, as let’s be honest, Intel is on its deathbed. But then you look at the price of a prebuilt, and think you can do better. No worries! You open up EPurchase to find that certain components have gone up in price drastically, most noticeably graphics cards and processors. This is the pandemic silicon shortage. How did this happen? Well, manufacturers made a wrong estimate of how many graphics cards or processors people would buy, and now they can’t keep up with the demand the lockdown has created. But wait, you don’t need the newest specs, right? Older machines are still good enough to run AAA games, just at lower settings. Sadly, due to this shortage, two groups have emerged that are determined to make you suffer. The miners and the scalpers. Miners, or specifically cryptocurrency miners are buying up graphics cards, both new and old to use to mine cryptocurrency. Currently, crypto coins such as Bitcoin or the more common to mine Ethereum are very valuable, making for a good investment opportunity (for the time being). These guys will stop at nothing to get some fake coins, so they can spend their lives in riches (if you can’t tell there’s a massive asterisk above the riches). The other group is scalpers, who, seeing that graphics cards are in high demand, buy up lots of them to resell to you at a higher price. They also do this with gaming consoles, so good luck trying to get a PS5. You close down all tabs on your search engine, and begin to cry, as your old Thinkpad isn’t able to replace the beast of a gaming rig that you could have. You wail and scream, cursing the people responsible. But then you remember. There’s another side to all of this. A side coated in aluminum housing, with little upgradability and prices higher than many mountains. Apple. Your friends warn you about the cost of these machines and their poor performance, how MacOS is impossible to use, but you ignore them. You are willing to sacrifice your morals and sanity for friendship. You gaze into the white light of the search engine, and with one tiny click of your broken trackpad, you enter the devil’s domain.


You arrive at a strange website, full of renders that try to get you to become a capitalist, to give them all of your money. One can gaze at the AirPods and the iPhones and be hypnotized, buying into the entire ecosystem instantly. However, your mission is to locate the Macs, which is the computer division of this massive company. The cursor, your starship begins to land on the Mac button, which leads you to your destination. However, my dear traveller, you have to choose your computer. The colorful iMac, with an integrated display, thinner than a sheet of paper. The Macbook’s, both Pro and Air that reign supreme over the laptop world. You begin to consider your option, but then something else catches your eye. A sleek, gray box, with the black bitten apple symbol on top. The Mac Mini, smallest of the new Macs, the one that everyone forgets about. It’s staring at you. Out of curiosity, you pick it up. Turning it around reveals one of its major advantages. The amount of ports. Sure, it is not the best, but still it offers so much more. Still, you step back. You remember all of those prices, inflated by the scourges of the internet, and you worry that Apple is also vulnerable. But then, you see it. The cost, while still being pretty expensive, is reasonable. You go to the Wayback Machine, to check the cost beforehand and, surprisingly, it had barely changed. How is this possible? You read more into the website, and find a crucial bit of information. In the ancient times, Intel reigned supreme over the processor manufacturer world. AMD couldn’t keep up, and even if they did, the partnership Intel had with Apple would still make them superior. But, that didn’t last long. The world began to change, and Ryzen processors became superior. Intel was losing support, but they still had their partner, right? Well, not really. After being fooled, burnt and humiliated by the 14 nanometer structure of the processors, Apple was making plans to create their own framework. Not limited to the poor architecture of x86, but to ascend to ARM. And so, in 2020, after being beaten by AMD to the point in which Intel would have to sell their soul to pay off the hospital bills, Apple came and with no remorse, pulled the trigger. They had created the M1 chip, the same one which the Apple website now offers. Realising this, you return to the board of ideas, and begin thinking about what you need from a computer. A big hard drive is the first thing that comes to mind, and you decide that you are a good file manager, and only need one terabyte. You’re also pretty smart, and realise that with a bigger drive, comes the need to access that date more quickly, and so you upgrade to sixteen gigabytes of RAM. The last choice is whether to get Final Cut Pro or not. You decide not to, as it’s too expensive, and you’re also not a streamer or a youtuber. After noting all of this down, you go to configure all of the Macs, and upon seeing the results you seem shocked.

The Mac Mini, which just looks like a small aluminum box, comes out on top. Let me explain. There are four options you can choose from, each having their advantages and disadvantages. The iMac has an included screen, keyboard and mouse, but has a really poor port selection, and the chin is far too large. And besides, the peripherals you get are horrible compared to others. The MacBook Air seems like a good option, until you realise that it overheats constantly. The MacBook Pro has good cooling, but is far too expensive and has the strangest design of all time, with absurdly gigantic speakers. The Mac Mini takes the cake. First, it has not only USB 3.0, but also gigabit ethernet straight out of the box, default configuration. Second, it does have a fan on the back, which will prevent it overheating too much. Third, it’s the cheapest. But what about accessories? Well, displays aren’t that expensive, you can get a good one for around 250 dollars, maybe even 200, and keyboard and mice are not as expensive as you think. You can get a Keychron K2 for around 100 dollars, and mice are far cheaper. Besides, in terms of specs, the Mac Mini offers the same ones, as the chipset is shared. But it has one crucial benefit. It is the only M1 Mac to support two external displays, while the others can only support one. You stand satisfied, with the mission accomplished, you go to check out. You have ordered the computer, and shortly after you buy all the peripherals. Grinning like a serial killer seeing teenage love, you sit back on your cheap chair and finally think about the freedom of having a modern computer. But then, you feel a sharp pain in your chest. Greed. You return to the Apple website, and begin looking at the other offers. The iPhone, the smart father of all smart devices has a new model, and it’s about time you upgrade. The AirPods Pro have amazing sound, and noise cancelling that can stop your sanity from telling you to go back, you add them to your cart. The iPad, the world's best tablet, with the same chip as your computer, and you know where it is? In your cart. The Pro Display XDR, along with it’s silly magnetic stand, it’s in your cart, you don’t look at specs anymore. You have been consumed, your remaining sanity is kept in a prison of aluminum and carbon, forced to watch in horror as you fill up cart, after cart, after cart. You don’t resemble yourself anymore. You have transformed yourself into a deformed creature, seven feet tall with long fingers and dry skin, that covers your slim, barely existent body. Your back has spikes, jutting from your skeleton, that if anyone touches, they die immediately. Your face can’t be seen, not because of the light, but because it is gone. Your lack of social status, your lack of friends, the fact that you don’t leave your room has turned your face into a smooth, skin plate, with a Apple logo crudely carved. Breathing from the holes in your chest, you buy every product on the website, official or not. Hunters and policemen have been hired to kill you, but they can’t. You’ve gained immortality, and from the stories of your murders, historical eternity. Is there a cure, can you save yourself? No, buy. Buy more, buy everything ten, hell, ten thousand times over. Just ****ing do it.

Thanks for reading this thread. It was a very fun thing to write, and I loved every second of it. I do really recommend the Mac Mini, and don’t worry, you won’t become the person in the story, unless you throw away your own opinions and only listen to others. Also, comment what phone you have,as I will use this info later. So, Happy Halloween.

ok for those not wanting to read through an m1 fan fiction here's the tl;dr of why the m1 mac mini is good/bad:
-has an included, keyboard and mouse
-poor port selection (idk not really for apple)
-looks sleek
-usb 3.0 ports(nothing new nor special)
-gigabit ethernet(nothing new nor special)
-can support 2 displays(nothing new nor special)
-m1 chip is powerful
-quiet

However that being said the m1 mac mini is a beast of a machine and makes more sense to buy than a full fat desktop with only integrated graphics. The m1 beats the i7 10700k DESKTOP, especially since it has thunderbolt ports you can always plug in an external gpu or get dongles for more usb ports. Overall for it's price it's actually worth buying compared to a desktop and even better for university students! Do note this is for work, not gaming though.
