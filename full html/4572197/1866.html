hello, i have brought together some stories i have experienced over the past few weeks.

1. exposed forum account

around the first or second day of school, a few weeks back, i did my own thing up until when i saw my friend, who is also a forumer on this website. he had mentioned my daily dose series and wanted to say how much he loved it ever since day 1. i really appreciated this and gave him my thanks.

this part of the story goes back to april where i had just reached 100 days of the series. another one of my friends had told my english teacher about the random facts series since it was related to the topic the class was discussing. as embarrassed as i was, the teacher said how that was pretty cool and moved on.

back to a few weeks ago. my friend was not in that class nor knew my forums account, which can only mean the entire school knows this account exists. but no one cares which is sick.

2. track

my stupid *** self thought it was a good idea to do gym this year for some reason, for a little backstory before you make fun of my stupidity.

a few weeks ago, our gym instructor wanted us to run a mile in under 15 mins. with the thought of 15 mins in my mind, i didn’t think it would be too hard. i still knew i sucked at running, but i didn’t know i sucked as much as you’re going to see.

when we got on the track ready to do the 3 laps, i realized my error with not choosing study hall.

umm, basically…

…

i walked the whole way.

i tried jogging, but got tired instantly so i just walked the 3 laps in 14:40, 20 seconds under the limit. lucky? beyond that.

what i noticed is how this guy just ran the entire thing, no breaks. he finished in like 5-6 mins with the support of usain bolt holy ****.

i do unfortunately have to redo the test since i didn’t get under 7 mins.

3. the hunt for food

a quick side note for people who are wondering why i can’t find food. it’s because i’m allergic to pretty much everything so i’m left with a limited supply of options. those options were what i was looking for.

another thing: the campus is, like, REALLY wide, so i walked like the distance to the moon

8,095 steps.

when i got out of class, i wanted to see if i could find food to eat, yknow, like any human being. i started my quest at the cafeteria, but had no luck. everything had cheese on it and i stalled lines asking.

i got outside and basically already gave up, but started my hunt 3 mins later.

i walked around 4,000 steps to another stand which only had stuff i was allergic to ofc. i swear, you would think in 2021 people evolve with food and don’t put ****ing milk and cheese in every ****ing product.

anyway, i had like 15 mins left of lunch so i walked back to the cafeteria and went for round two. i luckily happened to see something I WASN’T ALLERGIC TOO HOLY **** ISN’T THAT SOME LUCK??? it was some luck. i took that luck and slapped it on the floor after getting a sandwich with basically only bread xd.

i’m jk, the sandwich had ham on it which was really cool and yummy. the people taking my order of only a ham sandwich looked rather shocked as i stood there holding my phone crying inside.

i had like 4 mins to eat, but i ate it within that time and got to my next class tired af.

4. actually decent grades

the entirety of last year, i struggled with every single class. i managed to pass all my classes with a C average. my literature teacher was a blessing to ignore that grade and keep me in honors despite me almost failing the class. i don’t know how i feel about it since i see kids who got A’s in the previous class in the same class as me this year. they worked much harder than me and yet they are placed with me.

ig they do have the additional benefit of being in a class with such a swagtastic person

anyway, i got a 100 on a math test, 90 on a science quiz, and an 82 on a literature quiz.

pretty sick grades coming from someone who sucks at testing.


that concludes this thread. have a nice rest of your day and shak gives you good luck!

i have some extra short stories, but i’ll leave those for another time.
