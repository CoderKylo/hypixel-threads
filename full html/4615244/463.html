How To Make Top Tier High Quality Posts

Introduction​
I've seen quite a few new people join over the year and trying to express their thoughts but never finding the right way to do it causing an amazing idea with such high potential to just flop and get buried in hours. Even I've made these types of posts and have learned what separates those high quality and high tier posts to those lower quality posts that get far less attention even though the idea could be brilliant. I myself have made quite a few successful posts on here but just as many unsuccessful ones so here is my secret.​

Let's get an idea of what some of my most successful posts were:
The forumer pack
Why Hypixels Anti-Cheat Is Fundamentally Flawed
Why Freelook is an unfair advantage (bedwars)
Why Freelook is an unfair advantage (off topic)
Free Discord Nitro​

These were all uploaded at great times, while something was popular and trendy or involved many people thus meaning it stayed relevant for long periods of time.​

Step One, Getting Our Idea​
First step is writing down your idea. So say for example my idea is making a post about How To Make Top Tier High Quality Posts. Pretty good idea but we have to quantify it some way. The way we can do this is break this idea into some questions that can guide us to improve the idea or see if it's worthwhile to do. Let's also answer them with our example of "How To Make Top Tier High Quality Posts".​

1. Who is the target audience?
Well our target audience would be anyone new in the forums or to a younger audience looking to improve their posts.

2. Who is on right now?
Well right now I'm writing this at 12:40am so it's quite late/early for est and early morning for Europe time. This indicates it's not really peak hours so there will be little traction until the American powerhouse gets back on. Plus it's on a weekday so most action will happen either in the morning, during lunch or after school/work hours.

3. How popular is this topic?
This idea is pretty popular as there are more and more new people posting and this post could be easily applied to many other subforums. However there were much more new comers in the summer time so would get more traction posting then.

4. Has this been done already?
To my knowledge this idea has been touched upon but not as in depth.

5. How interesting is this idea?
This post could be interesting to anyone but would be most useful to new comers or a younger audience trying to make some quality posts. Could also show some more active users how to spice up their posts.

Step Two, Formulating Our Draft​
So now we got out questions done, awesome! Our next step is to analyze these questions and see what we can gather.
From analyzing the questions I did we can see that this is a pretty new idea that hasn't really been done, the popularity of the concept has dwindled a bit and lastly that when the post would go up it might be quite late so it will have trouble getting traction but may still interest people.

However let's say after taking a look at the answers you see that the post might not be too successful or the idea has already been done, then let's take a look and see what could be improved.​
​
  • If your target audience is quite narrow you could broaden your question or adjust it to include more people.​
  • If it's when it's off hours you could wait a few hours to post on peak hours.​
  • If the topic is not popular it's hard to make a topic popular so you have to try to make that topic interesting but also be able to get attention.​
  • If the post has already been done many many times it's best to change up the idea to something that hasn't been done as much yet as people will get bored of repetitive posts.​
  • If the idea doesn't look interesting anymore, try to see ways you could spice it up or add some flare.​
  • If the idea is popular and has gotten overwhelming negative reaction from the public try to see why that is and see how your opinion differs.​
  • Check if the post is also within the guidelines of the forums!​

Now that you we decided on a topic that we deem to be successful and lets write up a rough draft covering all the ideas we want to cover. This allows us to create nice and smooth flow from idea to idea. It makes reading and digesting the post so much easier thus creating better communication and less confusion.​

Step Three, Working On Our Final Copy​
So now that we have a rough draft written up with all our ideas it's time to do the dreaded final copy followed by proof reading it. Using things such as Grammarly, re-reading and other writing techniques can really help make the post that much better and professional showing that time went into making the post. This should also take a while and try to avoid rushing, you can end up leaving silly mistakes or creating confusing sentences that will have readers scratching their heads. Try to also avoid using complex words as no one wants to read a giant post full of legal rubbish.​

Step Four, Spicing Things Up​
Now that we have a final copy done lets spice it up with some visuals and also some nice text formatting. This can help the user digest a post much much better and make it feel much nicer to read while also looking professional. After all, who likes reading a 2k word essay with just text, this will bore people out so adding visuals can help keep that attention. Using text formatting such as even justifying the text can make it look a lot better and complete.

Now that we have an awesome looking post sometimes it's also sometimes cool to add a secret message/ easter egg. It may not always work but in this case it could work well. The secret message/easter egg could be that I made a post about How To Make Top Tier High Quality Posts while using a How To Make Top Tier High Quality Posts example. Pretty creative lol.​
​
Hopefully this helps you make better forum posts and get those views and reactions. Feel free to leave replies and I'll try to answer them to the best of my ability but other prominent forumers feel free to reply if I haven't had a chance yet!
If you want an idea of how long it took to make a post like this, it took about 1 hour.​

This post could be interesting to anyone but would be most useful to new comers or a younger audience trying to make some quality posts. Could also show some more active users how to spice up their posts.

​
  • If your target audience is quite narrow you could broaden your question or adjust it to include more people.​

  • If the topic is not popular it's hard to make a topic popular so you have to try to make that topic interesting but also be able to get attention.​

  • If the idea doesn't look interesting anymore, try to see ways you could spice it up or add some flare.​
  • If the idea is popular and has gotten overwhelming negative reaction from the public try to see why that is and see how your opinion differs.​
  • Check if the post is also within the guidelines of the forums!​
