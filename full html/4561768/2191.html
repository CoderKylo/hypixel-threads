This thread is just problems I have
This isn't specifically about this section, some of these are from other sections that I cba to go visit again


1. Toxicity.
Toxicity is very common on the forums, especially skyblock. It isn't really a problem here from what i've seen but I also don't visit here too often, but I remember when I made my 6969 milestone about 2 years ago ppl were toxic af about it for some reason.

2. People judge off pfps.
I swap pfps to this. No big deal right?
WRONG. Tens of people have told me to swap back to grass block pfp, even though I've said multiple times that I won't. Why does it matter to you what pfp I use? Do you have a problem with me being myself? Do you want to force me to be old me despite old me being cringe af and **** (@Pugmints can confirm).

3. The community likes to harass people for no reason.
This one I hope you guys don't do, as I know many forum communities do this. I and many others have been a huge target of harassment from forum communities which is not cool, however it has seemingly (omg tonyxd skyblock guild reference?!/1?!?1?1?/1) died down.

4. People take jokes to seriously.
Quite a lot of people know I jokingly say "weeb bad" on people's profiles, however some people seem to take that too seriously and then harass me for having this pfp. Anime pfp =/= weeb, and no anime pfp =/= not weeb.

4.5. People don't know what weeb means.
Weeb does not mean liking anime or anything. Weeb has always been and will always be a term for people who like Japanese culture so much they wish they were Japanese. This isn't a reason to discriminate against them, people can like what they want to, but that also means 99% of "weebs" in this community aren't weebs.

5. People aren't original.
When was the last time 69/420 was funny? When was the last time Jerry was funny? Stop making these jokes, they aren't funny. This is a topic for another thread for SGD which I am going to make, if you would like to be pinged when it comes out lmk.

6. Getting recognized.
Back when I had nota_noob as my ign, multiple people would come up to me and ask "are you the guy i saw on the forums?," but also people then go up to me on the forums and ask "are you the guy who killed me in skywars?" (yes I play skywars if you didn't know) which gets kinda annoying if I'm trying to have a serious discussion, especially if it's not in the skywars forums.

7. Just the state of the forums.
The amount of low-effort unfunny ****posts is through the roof. The fact that the demand for low effort unoriginal discussion threads has almost passed the demand for effort threads is just insane. This is the only section I even think about visiting if I don't want to lose brain cells, yet even then I still lose them looking at the front page.

7.5. Mislabeling effort posts.
Just because your thread has 2 paragraphs doesn't mean it's an effort post. An effort post has multiple paragraphs, spoilers/sections, at least 2k words, ect. A high effort thread isn't a 3 paragraph with 2 images thread, it's an entire presentation basically. I don't see people misuse these very often as people don't put effort into posts anymore.


I don't know how to write conclusions so...
Feedback appreciated!

just my take on some of the stuff, tried to make it interesting too

First one is on toxicity, this unfortunately is part of the internet and just something people have to deal with. It's just generally better to stay away from those things because it can rub you in a way that you might start to act the same way. People say skyblock is quite toxic and while I have seen that there are also countless many good people who want to help. It's just tuning out those people.

Second one is profile pictures and people judging them. This one is quite lengthy but really drives the point home with irl examples, YALL BETTER NOT TAKE THIS OUT OF CONTEXT TOO!
This is something you actually can't do anything about as this is how the human brain is wired, it will naturally have a bias whether big or small is dependent on the person but this cannot be changed.
Here's an example, you go to a club and you see some guys or girls and more often than not your brain is thinking who's the hottest of the bunch. Maybe from the girls there is Lana Cloud, Riley Sineur, Rebecca roadam (Not real people, just examples) but right away without even knowing their names nor personalities your brain goes for the best looking one. Whoever that is depends on each person, one person might prefer blond hair and another brown. Someone else might want a tall girl or someone else a short, it depends per person.
Same with forum profiles, everyone will automatically judge based on what they see and what they think. For example someone looking at an anime profile might think "hey that's cool ik that character" or "what the hell is wrong with this person, why do they fantasize girl cartoon drawing so much". Two different people, same image, complete different answers and you can't change that as that's how the brain works and it will always have a bias!

For harassments it's similar to toxicity where it usually has to do with what communities you're in, communities that are toxic will engage in toxic behaviour. best to avoid those

Jokes are nice to see and whatnot but sometimes if multiple people don't get them then you most likely stepped over the line. This is also counterintuitive to your toxicity point because such jokes can be seen as toxic or vice versa were toxic comments could originally be meant as jokes. It's either best to go with something non offensive, light hearted or easy to figure out. For example making jokes to delete system32 on a help thread is a joke but can also be seen as toxic and showing disrespect to a player for their lack of knowledge. It's also annoying.

The weeb part is confusing because you say it means people who are obsessed with Japanese culture and their content but then go to saying most people who are called weebs aren't? So then what are they? Most people get called weebs for watching anime and enjoying that which could defenitly fall in being obsessed with Japanese content thus making them weebs?

The next thing you mentioned was originality, while this is true you also have to help guide the new generation of people and show them how to be original. Not just be pessimistic and say you guys are bad and can't come up with original content, instead you have to leave constructive criticism and show people how to make their posts more interesting and have that uniqueness to them. This is like a teacher saying your story is bad and a waste of time, it discourages you from trying more and potentially becoming a great writer and leaves a bad taste in your mouth, instead a teacher who helps you get your ideas out and show you how to do it might leave a much better impact and makes you feel better about yourself and you feel encourages to keep trying until you get it right. After all if we dig through people's old posts they most likely will be considered unoriginal and bad. But as time goes on and other forumers leave opinions you get better and better.

Getting recognized is something you just have to live with when being well known on the forums. Instead of looking at it being annoying you should embrace it and make those people feel even better. Happiness can easily spread so it can make you feel better too.

State of forums is pretty much same thing as originality, some people think that this is the way and a way to get well known. You should also look at who's posting these threads, these are more often than not young kids who want to leave a positive impact but people who have a different view on it trash it and say a bunch of negative stuff. It leaves a bad taste and causes people to switch paths. I'd rather have bad forum discussion threads than none so at least people can learn from the past.

High effort does not mean it needs to have 2k+ words and all those bells and whistles, that's not true. Instead it should have quality content that is written concisely with correct grammar and accurately describe their idea. After all if this was the case I'd put copy pasting 2k word recipes on here everyday, be putting in spoilers and text formatting everywhere and I'd have the most high quality content possible and I'd overpass sykese in a matter of weeks. From this alone you can see that it simply doesn't hold up true. On top of that very little people here have the attention span to read a 2k word post, even I haven't gotten to writing that much and I can barely read through it. Instead you should be looking at quality of the work, not the quantity of it.

That's my take on it
