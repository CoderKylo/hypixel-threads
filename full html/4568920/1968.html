Okay so I've seen several threads on people asking for advice on this topic, so here:

So you made a thread about how to get a gf. There's a surprisingly good amount of advice on that topic for a forum for a block game. I'm going to go through some tips, speaking from experience. I'm also told I'm good at relationship advice, having helped several people through things such as Heartbreak, *******, Depression, Anxiety, and more.

Okay so here are my points

This is the new part, added now. Most of the stuff here is from several months ago, but it's still valid. If you haven't already seen it, I suggest reading it.

1) If it's your first relationship, don't be too pressuring on them. Say you're around 13, 14. It's your first real relationship. I myself fell into this trap, I was so desperate for attention and love, that I pressured her into thinking we had to do certain things, and that ended in an abrupt end to my feelings. However this is about you, not me. Don't pressure them, take it slow and steady, there's plenty of time left in your life to do everything everyone else does.

2) If she says no, don't ask again. Even if it's like, 3 years later. It's never a good idea, and any chance of a friendship sparking later is gone. The bridge is burnt.

3) Don't date someone just for looks, please. Looks mean the least in a relationship. Go for personality.

4) From @ShiftyWifty
It helps to know yourself before jumping into a relationship. Otherwise you'll struggle to keep yourself afloat through it all.

This isn't necessarily about being fake, but rather knowing who you are, and who you are not. You might not know certain parts of yourself yet, and while that is fine as you are likely young it does help immeasurably when you work to figure these parts out as they will always come up in a relationship at any given time.

5) From @CrazyKing54
I invited my girlfriend to my server (not an advertisement, I don't even run it anymore) and then dated her for over a year. Still going, flew down to see her for the first time in July. Looking to propose to her next year. My best advice is do not do anything impure, or that you would regret. Keep clear communication. Apologize if you do something wrong, talk through issues and respect boundaries. Spend time together, even if it is something mundane like helping her study or letting her listen to you read your work aloud. Hold yourself to the same, or higher, standards that you hold your partner to. Relationships that are built on respect, patience, purity, clear and healthy communication, and other virtues, will prosper. Less heartache, less pain. And if you don't fool around before marriage, there is a very high chance you will remain committed, and never divorce. Follow a noble path, and don't trade away a long, lasting relationship for short term pleasure or emotional outbursts.

6) For gay males
By @Wholesome
I'm only really speaking from my own experiences and knowledge, so this could easily vary depending on the person.

Gay relationships are much stranger to deal with than straight ones, in most cases.

To start, it's impossible to tell who is gay. There's always jokes about the gaydar, but it's almost never accurate. Honestly, a lot of the problems with gay relationships stems from confidence and a big game of "who's what?".

Just for reference, I don't recommend going up to some fellow and asking if they're gay.

If you like someone, get to know them, even a little. No matter how flustered you get talking to someone, it's always good to know the guy's personality and interests, and what they like. Play into it. Most guys will probably figure out you like them if you're acting all weak and stuff, so you'll need to be at the very least confident enough to either not care or work past that. Some other things I would like to add are that you do NOT need to be out to everyone to try to get a relationship. Just make sure the other guy doesn't run his mouth about it.
Personally, I do recommend being publicly out, though, as it's much easier to find relationships and deal with people without having to hide that main bit if yourself.

Also. Compliments go a long way for guys, we almost never get complimented.
Just as an example. I've personally got interests in games like Persona, and also like to delve into fashion (mostly of my own). If a guy ever compliments me on my clothing or hair I usually get quite high opinions of them.

Can't find a guy?

If your school has a GSA, join it. Often will have some guys there you could talk to. Nice groups. Also look for school discords and other group channels, plus just whatever hallway gossip groups you can find (straight girls are pretty good at finding gay guys, just a weird bit of advice)
In my own experiences, I've about asked out three different guys and the only things stopping anything from happening have been my lack of confidence and their own relationship status.

7) From @Solosheeep
Also, it is important that you get to know the girl first. Everyone acts different at home than they do in public. So get to know her first. This is also out of respect for the girl. If a guy asked me I without getting to know me, I would feel kinda angry. The point of dating is to find a spouse. If you start dating someone just by there looks or what you THINK is his/her personality is (yes, this goes for girls too), you will regret it. But overall this is a great thread. A bit weird it's on the Hypixel forums. But, yeah. Good thread





This is a bit of a mess so if you have any questions talk to me on discord @sparkon#0001 (won't be on forums much, so don't DM me here)



I'm seeing a few posts regarding asking friends for help. This doesn't work as often as people think it does. Usually your friend group will support whatever you are saying or feeling. They will rarely ever provide a different outlo0k on your struggles. This is where talking to a person who does not know your troubles is more beneficial

That's all I have to say for now, good luck!



How to approach her ( suggested by @uNerdd)

Don't just walk up and ask her out right there. Most girls don't like it thrown on them, it just makes it awkward and weird, and it's very likely she'll say no, especially if you're not already acquainted. Become his/her friend first, chances are one of you will gain feelings, and your chances are so much higher if you're already friends. (Thanks @Azuko)
Further from the point above, try to be friendly and create a friendship with her first. It makes it so much easier if she's already your friend, that way it's not too awkward.
Don't over-do it. No need to buy her a Lambo and display the message in the sky made of clouds. Just make it simple, and try not to do it over text-message.
Don't wait for her. If you think she's into you as well, don't wait for her to ask you. Shoot your shot, give it a go, she'll say yes if she likes you
How to maintain a relationship

Be yourself. This is my best advice. What's the point of a relationship if it's not real? Don't act like your the best person in the world, don't be over-affectionate (Unless she likes that kinda thing), just be yourself. That's the very best advice I can give you.
Don't try too hard. Of course, try, do your best, just... don't go to the point where it's costing you two hours to get dressed in a $500 tuxedo for a date. That's a bit overkill (It's also an exaggeration, but still)

DON'T LIE. Don't keep anything from her. By lying your effectively ending the relationship right there, because she's eventually gonna find out and guess what happens when she does. Now, it depends on the severity and seriousness of the lie, but nothing good will come out of it, that's for sure.

Don't pressure her to do anything. Speaking from experience, it doesn't go well. If you want to kiss, ask her, don't just do it. No-one likes that kind of thing spontaneously happening.
More advice from @tyrantlemon55

Girls, guys, non-binary folk, we're all human - and we all have human emotions. If you want to get into a relationship - even a friendship, you have to remember the humanity of the other person - they have feelings, too! Generally, treat others the way you want to be treated. If you are kind, true to yourself, and interesting, people will want to be around you.



Don't be desperate when looking. If you seem like you are just trying to get into a relationship for the sake of it, it won't work. People will just think that you're desperate.



Respect rejection. If you get rejected, take a look at yourself. What can YOU do better? Sure, there might be some people who only care about a confession if the other person is good-looking or rich, but those guys are not the kind of people you want to date. If you know the other person is not that kind of person, then NEVER blame them for getting rejected. Think about what you can improve, and how you will be able to grow from it. Unless their feelings change, don't try again too quickly as that will make you more annoying and desperate in their eyes. If they are your friend, don't let the friendship die from it! It might be awkward, but once you both move past it, you'll have a more honest friendship. If they reject you as they are not interested in your gender, also don't hold it against them.



Be interesting. People will like you more if you are social. Have defined interests that are well-rounded. Be open-minded and not one-note. Put in some effort, too! People will be more inclined to be around a speedrunner for Minecraft than a Skyblock sweat. In that regard, use social media as a way to share your passions. Just... don't get too sucked into it and remember that it's not an accurate representation of other people's lives. Don't be afraid to have "abnormal" interests like toy collecting or crocheting. Take it in stride, and respect the interests of others, too!



Love yourself. The keystone is self-confidence. Know that you're flawed and that you aren't perfect - and that's okay. You should accept yourself for who you are. Don't be afraid to change yourself by working to rectify those flaws, though! Loving yourself also means taking interest in bettering your mind and body. Take mental health days, practice good hygiene, watch what you eat, get into working out, and learn something fun!

Further Advice from @LowestStone

Something I feel is worth adding is to be as honest and forthcoming as can be. You tacitly mention it with your first point and directly in your third, but I don't think this can be stressed enough. I've found through personal experience that being as genuine and honest as possible will get you the farthest.



Don't pretend you're something you are not. If you keep up appearances and try go latch onto the first opportunity that comes along, you'll find yourself in an incompatible match and unhappy. Being fake is only going to lead to a fake relationship.



Yeah, maybe you have a personality or traits that are considered "less attractive", but ask yourself would you rather wait for someone who is willing to work around that or help you overcome that, or thinly veil it for someone who is inevitably going to see right through it? Everyone is flawed... Everyone. We've all done something that someone else would consider "bad", "immoral", "stupid", and whatever substitute you wish to insert. There's no shame in being honest with others that we are human and have messed up; just as long as a commitment is made to learn from our mistakes.



Another thing I feel is worth mentioning is that relationships aren't always going to be sunshine and rainbows all the time. At one point or another, you are going to run into a disagreement, or have resentment towards the other person; no matter how good the relationship is. Be prepared to face these head-on and work them out in a mature and constructive manner that helps the situation. I can't give great advice as every experience in this sense is individualâbut try your best to understand the other perspective, and recognize when maybe time away from each other might be needed to not act in impulse or emotionâlike a happening with a parent, sibling, or friend. Also recognize that these are mere speed-bumps, nothing to constantly look back, reflect on, or get upset at once all is said and done.

I just wanted to add those. You present very good advice, and I hope that at least one person is helped out by it. Even if the forum is for a block game, it's nice to people taking time to post more real topics that concern things we fear or feel we need help on.

TLDR: Don't be too forward with it. Don't over-do it. Don't wait for her, Don't try too hard. Don't Lie. Don't pressure her. Respect Rejection. Be interesting. Love yourself. Don't pretend your something your not.

Those are my points

If you need anything else or advice for a specific scenario, tell me below.

Okay so I've seen several threads on people asking for advice on this topic, so here:

So you made a thread about how to get a gf. There's a surprisingly good amount of advice on that topic for a forum for a block game. I'm going to go through some tips, speaking from experience. Okay so here are my points

This is the new part, added now. Most of the stuff here is from several months ago, but it's still valid. If you haven't already seen it, I suggest reading it.

1) If it's your first relationship, don't be too pressuring on them. Say you're around 13, 14. It's your first real relationship. I myself fell into this trap, I was so desperate for attention and love, that I pressured her into thinking we had to do certain things, and that ended in an abrupt end to my feelings. However this is about you, not me. Don't pressure them, take it slow and steady, there's plenty of time left in your life to do everything everyone else does.

2) If she says no, don't ask again. Even if it's like, 3 years later. It's never a good idea, and any chance of a friendship sparking later is gone. The bridge is burnt.

3) Don't date someone just for looks, please. Looks mean the least in a relationship. Go for personality.

That's all I have to say for now, good luck!



How to approach her ( suggested by @uNerdd)

Don't just walk up and ask her out right there. Most girls don't like it thrown on them, it just makes it awkward and weird, and it's very likely she'll say no, especially if you're not already acquainted. Become his/her friend first, chances are one of you will gain feelings, and your chances are so much higher if you're already friends. (Thanks @Azuko)
Further from the point above, try to be friendly and create a friendship with her first. It makes it so much easier if she's already your friend, that way it's not too awkward.
Don't over-do it. No need to buy her a Lambo and display the message in the sky made of clouds. Just make it simple, and try not to do it over text-message.
Don't wait for her. If you think she's into you as well, don't wait for her to ask you. Shoot your shot, give it a go, she'll say yes if she likes you
How to maintain a relationship

Be yourself. This is my best advice. What's the point of a relationship if it's not real? Don't act like your the best person in the world, don't be over-affectionate (Unless she likes that kinda thing), just be yourself. That's the very best advice I can give you.
Don't try too hard. Of course, try, do your best, just... don't go to the point where it's costing you two hours to get dressed in a $500 tuxedo for a date. That's a bit overkill (It's also an exaggeration, but still)

DON'T LIE. Don't keep anything from her. By lying your effectively ending the relationship right there, because she's eventually gonna find out and guess what happens when she does. Now, it depends on the severity and seriousness of the lie, but nothing good will come out of it, that's for sure.

Don't pressure her to do anything. Speaking from experience, it doesn't go well. If you want to kiss, ask her, don't just do it. No-one likes that kind of thing spontaneously happening.
More advice from @tyrantlemon55

Girls, guys, non-binary folk, we're all human - and we all have human emotions. If you want to get into a relationship - even a friendship, you have to remember the humanity of the other person - they have feelings, too! Generally, treat others the way you want to be treated. If you are kind, true to yourself, and interesting, people will want to be around you.



Don't be desperate when looking. If you seem like you are just trying to get into a relationship for the sake of it, it won't work. People will just think that you're desperate.



Respect rejection. If you get rejected, take a look at yourself. What can YOU do better? Sure, there might be some people who only care about a confession if the other person is good-looking or rich, but those guys are not the kind of people you want to date. If you know the other person is not that kind of person, then NEVER blame them for getting rejected. Think about what you can improve, and how you will be able to grow from it. Unless their feelings change, don't try again too quickly as that will make you more annoying and desperate in their eyes. If they are your friend, don't let the friendship die from it! It might be awkward, but once you both move past it, you'll have a more honest friendship. If they reject you as they are not interested in your gender, also don't hold it against them.



Be interesting. People will like you more if you are social. Have defined interests that are well-rounded. Be open-minded and not one-note. Put in some effort, too! People will be more inclined to be around a speedrunner for Minecraft than a Skyblock sweat. In that regard, use social media as a way to share your passions. Just... don't get too sucked into it and remember that it's not an accurate representation of other people's lives. Don't be afraid to have "abnormal" interests like toy collecting or crocheting. Take it in stride, and respect the interests of others, too!



Love yourself. The keystone is self-confidence. Know that you're flawed and that you aren't perfect - and that's okay. You should accept yourself for who you are. Don't be afraid to change yourself by working to rectify those flaws, though! Loving yourself also means taking interest in bettering your mind and body. Take mental health days, practice good hygiene, watch what you eat, get into working out, and learn something fun!
