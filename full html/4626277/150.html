Forum - noun
A place, meeting, or medium where ideas and views on a particular issue can be exchanged.

This is the definition of the word 'forum' as according to a very fancy dictionary written by a famous man in the 1800s google.

I feel that the Hypixel forums are different and special, in a way that is not seen on most forums. It is unique in ways that keep drawing me towards it and allow me to enjoy my time spent on it.

Forums are a place to ask questions, yes. They are a place to discuss ideas, yes. But what makes me love the Hypixel forums so much? The closely bonded community.

The forums community is so closely related to one another. We all share many common interests and ideas. Just using forums allows us to connect with others. I feel that being connected to someone else by forums, or both of you being 'foruners,' is one of the strongest connections you can make while you are here on hypixel.

Sure, I meet people in game, in my guild, in random lobbies even. But the strongest connections I have made always started with forums. We forumers are so closely connected, but we don't even realize it. I bet I could reach out to one of you replying to this thread right now and play some games together, and we'd realize how similar we are and become good friends.

The forums are a place for us to be ourselves, which is why I believe we enjoy it so much here and have so much fun. In real life, we usually worry about what others think of us, heck, even in game we care about if our stats look good or not. Here? There's none of that, we are just who we truly are, which is one thing I find great about the forums.

I feel comfortable talking about a lot of things here than I do to even my real life friends as I know I won't be judged much for anything.

I also like how chill everyone is. Sure, there's the occasional crapposter, but when we do see one, we just laugh about it. Even the bad things that happen just turn into funny jokes we can look back on, which is another reason I like it here.

My Favorite Sections, and Why I Like Them:

Off Topic

This section, the off topic section, is definitely my favorite because we have no restrictions on what to talk about. There's so much variety in the content posted here that I like to just browse through and read what others have been up to. I also feel like out of all the sections, the people in this one are the most friendly and open to others. This is also mainly what I was referring to earlier about being comfortable with sharing ideas/experiences of mine. I have so many countless stories that I would never tell anywhere but here, and that's because I know the worst thing that can happen would be getting a good laugh out of it.

There are a few people here though that just really brighten up the mood here though, and it's people like these that are part of the reason I join and stay here. A few examples of people I look up to in this forum to are StevenR8, Alphacloud, and Tourtoise. I'm sure many of you can agree with me that cake man is just a great guy and a funny person. Alphacloud is like the friendly neighborhood tech support. While he himself might not be the most active poster or anything, the impact he has on this forum is actually quite hilarious. As for Tourtoise? He is a turtle. Period.

Community Help Forum

The community help forum is another one of my favorites. If I were to describe it in one word, it would be wholesome. It's your typical forum, where people ask questions, but the active people there are so nice and fun to hang out with. Everyone ther is so nice as well! When someone posts a thread, there's literally a race for who can type the fastest, and the thread poster gets a response almost always within a minute. There's almost no drama/toxicity/anything 'bad' really in this forum, it's just a place for those who like to help others to hang out, and a way to meet others who also like to help others.

Most of the time, on any competitive-style thing, leaderboarders for that will likely be playing mostly only because they want to maintain their leaderboard position. However, the great thing about the community help forum is that the solutions leaderboards are not like that. I'm telling you, pretty much everyone with a high solution count is there not because of the stat, but because they like helping people, and that genuine passion they have is one of the great things I like about the solutions community.

Some people I look up to in this forum are Aparting, Morelikes, Zolt, and Centranos. They all are great people and great to talk to, and we all share the common interest and goal of making another person's day better.

A question I get asked quite often is "hey mango, how do I get a lot of solutions?" Well, now that this is my 50 solutions special, I feel that I am somewhat qualified to answer that question (if this sounds egotistical, I can assure you it's not meant to). Simply put, those with higher solution counts usually care more about helping people rather than getting the stat numbers higher. Most people starting out care mostly about how many solutions they have and if they get their answer marked or not. Well, stop doing that. Start caring more about actually helping the person and helping them get through whatever problem they have, and that passion you have will show up and automatically get you more solutions. For me, the end goal isn't the solutions. It's making sure and feeling assured that the person got their question answered properly and thoroughly.

Bedwars

Ah yes, the wonderful world of the bedwars forums. If you've been there, you probably know that it doesn't seem like the type of forum I would like. However, I do appreciate it because of the humor. I feel that the bedwars community always has something funny to offer that'll make me laugh. Whenever I feel down, I will literally just go to the bedwars forum to cheer me up. There's always a dhar mann post or bedwars starter pack thread to get me chuckling.

I also like it for the reason that many people make achievement threads. Some people dislike achievement threads and justify their reasoning by saying "the achievement isn't impressive enough" or "these types of threads just clutter up the forums." Well, I don't look at it that way. What I may not consider impressive would still be a great accomplishment for someone else. I remember when I first hit 100 stars, I felt overjoyed. Now? I look at it as something that's just "cool." I recently hit 300 stars. For me, it was insane. For someone who was twice that much? Probably not that significant to them. It's all about perspective, so I still feel happy reading all the achievement threads because I know that the person who hit their milestone felt proud, and it makes me smile.

In this forum, some people I look up to are Melon, Panther, Husket, Dadje, and ThatDarnedDuck. I've played with most of them at least a few times, and I'm good friends with a few of them. They're all super fun people to hang out with and I like reading their threads.

Conclusion:

The Hypixel Forums are unlike amy other in the way that its community is so closely connected. The people here are great and fun to hang out with, and we all share common thoughts and interests which allow us to be ourselves and befriend one another. These reasons are what keep me on the forums, the close connection I have with many people. Now, why did I make this thread? I have absolutely no idea. I haven't really ever made a long thread before, and I recently hit 50 solutions, so I thought, why not? I hope you enjoyed reading this little article of my thoughts, and I hope you have a good day!

The forums community is so closely related to one another. We all share many common interests and ideas. Just using forums allows us to connect with others. I feel that being connected to someone else by forums, or both of you being 'foruners,' is one of the strongest connections you can make while you are here on hypixel.

Sure, I meet people in game, in my guild, in random lobbies even. But the strongest connections I have made always started with forums. We forumers are so closely connected, but we don't even realize it. I bet I could reach out to one of you replying to this thread right now and play some games together, and we'd realize how similar we are and become good friends.

The forums are a place for us to be ourselves, which is why I believe we enjoy it so much here and have so much fun. In real life, we usually worry about what others think of us, heck, even in game we care about if our stats look good or not. Here? There's none of that, we are just who we truly are, which is one thing I find great about the forums.

I feel comfortable talking about a lot of things here than I do to even my real life friends as I know I won't be judged much for anything.

I also like how chill everyone is. Sure, there's the occasional crapposter, but when we do see one, we just laugh about it. Even the bad things that happen just turn into funny jokes we can look back on, which is another reason I like it here.

My Favorite Sections, and Why I Like Them:

Off Topic

This section, the off topic section, is definitely my favorite because we have no restrictions on what to talk about. There's so much variety in the content posted here that I like to just browse through and read what others have been up to. I also feel like out of all the sections, the people in this one are the most friendly and open to others. This is also mainly what I was referring to earlier about being comfortable with sharing ideas/experiences of mine. I have so many countless stories that I would never tell anywhere but here, and that's because I know the worst thing that can happen would be getting a good laugh out of it.

There are a few people here though that just really brighten up the mood here though, and it's people like these that are part of the reason I join and stay here. A few examples of people I look up to in this forum to are StevenR8, Alphacloud, and Tourtoise. I'm sure many of you can agree with me that cake man is just a great guy and a funny person. Alphacloud is like the friendly neighborhood tech support. While he himself might not be the most active poster or anything, the impact he has on this forum is actually quite hilarious. As for Tourtoise? He is a turtle. Period.

Community Help Forum

The community help forum is another one of my favorites. If I were to describe it in one word, it would be wholesome. It's your typical forum, where people ask questions, but the active people there are so nice and fun to hang out with. Everyone ther is so nice as well! When someone posts a thread, there's literally a race for who can type the fastest, and the thread poster gets a response almost always within a minute. There's almost no drama/toxicity/anything 'bad' really in this forum, it's just a place for those who like to help others to hang out, and a way to meet others who also like to help others.

Most of the time, on any competitive-style thing, leaderboarders for that will likely be playing mostly only because they want to maintain their leaderboard position. However, the great thing about the community help forum is that the solutions leaderboards are not like that. I'm telling you, pretty much everyone with a high solution count is there not because of the stat, but because they like helping people, and that genuine passion they have is one of the great things I like about the solutions community.

Some people I look up to in this forum are Aparting, Morelikes, Zolt, and Centranos. They all are great people and great to talk to, and we all share the common interest and goal of making another person's day better.

A question I get asked quite often is "hey mango, how do I get a lot of solutions?" Well, now that this is my 50 solutions special, I feel that I am somewhat qualified to answer that question (if this sounds egotistical, I can assure you it's not meant to). Simply put, those with higher solution counts usually care more about helping people rather than getting the stat numbers higher. Most people starting out care mostly about how many solutions they have and if they get their answer marked or not. Well, stop doing that. Start caring more about actually helping the person and helping them get through whatever problem they have, and that passion you have will show up and automatically get you more solutions. For me, the end goal isn't the solutions. It's making sure and feeling assured that the person got their question answered properly and thoroughly.

Bedwars

Ah yes, the wonderful world of the bedwars forums. If you've been there, you probably know that it doesn't seem like the type of forum I would like. However, I do appreciate it because of the humor. I feel that the bedwars community always has something funny to offer that'll make me laugh. Whenever I feel down, I will literally just go to the bedwars forum to cheer me up. There's always a dhar mann post or bedwars starter pack thread to get me chuckling.

I also like it for the reason that many people make achievement threads. Some people dislike achievement threads and justify their reasoning by saying "the achievement isn't impressive enough" or "these types of threads just clutter up the forums." Well, I don't look at it that way. What I may not consider impressive would still be a great accomplishment for someone else. I remember when I first hit 100 stars, I felt overjoyed. Now? I look at it as something that's just "cool." I recently hit 300 stars. For me, it was insane. For someone who was twice that much? Probably not that significant to them. It's all about perspective, so I still feel happy reading all the achievement threads because I know that the person who hit their milestone felt proud, and it makes me smile.

In this forum, some people I look up to are Melon, Panther, Husket, Dadje, and ThatDarnedDuck. I've played with most of them at least a few times, and I'm good friends with a few of them. They're all super fun people to hang out with and I like reading their threads.

Conclusion:

The Hypixel Forums are unlike amy other in the way that its community is so closely connected. The people here are great and fun to hang out with, and we all share common thoughts and interests which allow us to be ourselves and befriend one another. These reasons are what keep me on the forums, the close connection I have with many people. Now, why did I make this thread? I have absolutely no idea. I haven't really ever made a long thread before, and I recently hit 50 solutions, so I thought, why not? I hope you enjoyed reading this little article of my thoughts, and I hope you have a good day!
