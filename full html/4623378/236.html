if sure google has answers to these. except 3. these questions are kinda sus put together.

for question 3, there are other factors that need to be considered. For example, amount of time exposed to a certain temperature. For example, at 200F, you would "lose the ability to see" (die) within a few minutes, but at 5000F, you would "lose the ability to see" (also die) very nearly instantly. Without a time variable, we have a wide range of possible answers.

Another thing is whether "no longer [being able to] see out of your eyes" means you don't see anymore because your eyeballs themselves get cooked first, or because you die of heat and are subsequently unable to "see". For example, at 200F, you would most likely die of heat stroke before your eyes lose the ability to receive and transmit visual stimuli. At 5000F, your eyes would most likely cook first, since you would be charred from the outside in (very quickly).
