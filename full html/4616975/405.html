On October 16, 2021, Mojang revealed the naming and some content of 1.19: The Wild Update. Piles of content are to be added, mostly affecting caves, swamps, and other features. But what's next for the game? Where are we now, where are we going, and what will happen next?

Where We Are Now
Every year for 5 years now, Microsoft have ran Minecraft Live, previously Minecon Earth, with a major focus on a vote of some sort, and updates. Starting with the voting, there are a few flaws. For one, there's the issue of losing content being discarded. There have been 7 mobs so far discarded from the game, and the winners are often looked upon with regret later. There's the influence of public figures to deal with, which some people say "ruined" 2020's Mob Vote. It took 3 years for the swamp to even be considered, and it'll be another year until its release. Many of us know or have an idea which mob was going to win; it was especially obvious with this year's. So what does this mean for the rest of Minecraft?

Blocks, Biomes, and Bad Decisions
All things end eventually. Ancient civilizations' rule over the land ended, 18th century business ventures ended, and today, games end. There's a possibility that Minecraft may stay for much longer, but that's unlikely. Of course, Minecraft has its own problems, and these may be the distant downfall of everyone's favorite voxel-based sandbox game. Multiplayer content is being neglected. Out of all the recent updates, only a few changes have been server-oriented: Bossbar coloring, structure blocks, and most recently the Java 16 upgrade, whose helpfulness is debated. Performance gets worse every update, and running servers is more and more unfeasible due to lacking plugins, bad TPS, and the competition of those P2W servers that never seem to lose players. Bedrock is still being pushed, despite things like Pixel Paradise failing. Mojang is pushing the "kids game" idea further, and it may be a possibility that chat filtering or even moderation is added to Java.
But that's the worst timeline. There's probably something better to be said about this.

Wait, that can't be the end!
The likely result is Mojang continues to keep everything alive in their Mojang-y spirit and the game continues for some time, until it gets its final update and gets open-sourced for everyone, including server developers, to play around with. Minecraft Dungeons is a great spin-off, adding some new content (that would feel at home in the base game too). Best of all, there's a great community who will keep being creative, with mods, resource packs, adventures, and more. It really seems like a game designed to stay a while (stay forever!) and remain a part of gaming culture for a long time after.

Infinite Possibilities
There's really no set track as to where Minecraft will go in the future. Servers keep inventing new things, and there's always something new to play. I'm personally thinking about a space exploration simulator built using plugins and custom chunk mechanisms. There's everything from SMPs to MMORPGs built using these blocks. Overall the outlook of this block game is quite bright, and I hope future content brings more cool stuff (and no glow squids).
I tried to make all the chapters reference a music disc, but the cat got my tongue.
