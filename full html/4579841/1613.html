Mobility and survival is much easier when you can see. If you can identify things, you can avoid hazards and find things that are helpful. If you don't like socializing, you can pretend (or legitimately not be able to) communicate with someone. Of course, being deaf has its downsides, like not being able to hear water, yelling, screaming etc, but you will be able to see dangerous or helpful people and resources. You can also entertain yourself a lot more visually, however that is a lesser issue. You could make the argument it would be better to be blind in a more non-material way though. When you can hear but not see, you do not make judgements on appearances. You can listen to others without judging how they look, and you can be a great comfort to someone who is anxious about their appearances. You can still listen to music, speeches, sermons and other culturally significant pieces of literature. You would be more in-tune with your spirit and emotions, whereas a deaf person would never be able to hear these things. A deaf person would have a lot harder of a time socially if they did not lip read or found emotions hard to comprehend.
