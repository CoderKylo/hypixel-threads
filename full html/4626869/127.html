The breakdown of bad apologies:
1. Insincere apologies: Ones where the person just says "I'm sorry" because they are forced to. Often said with a tinge of disgust or resentment. It is quite clear when one is given.
2. Dodging accusations: This can take many roles, like apologizing to something small, but ignoring the bigger aspect in hope that the smaller behaviour's apology is enough to stop the victim from asking for more. (Say you teased a kid and then punched him. You only apologize for the teasing, and he says it's ok. You never apologized for the assault, only the harassment, but he will not bother you anymore.)
3. Using emotionally charged language without addressing the issue at hand: People who are influencers often do this. "I was in a dark place" "I wasn't myself that day/night". Creating excuses and making appeals to emotion may look like remorse, but often it is a cheap tactic to take the offence/pain away from you and portray the person who needs to apologize as the victim.
4. But or however statements: These make whatever offence was made feel deserved or inevitable. "I didn't mean to punch you, but you were annoying me. I'm sorry" is a decent example. Usually, this tactic is used to make excuses for wronging someone.
5. Me first apologies: Any apology that puts the aggressor in the prime focus is not the greatest one. "I didn't mean to do that. I guess I am a jerk" and so on may look sincere as well, however it does little to address the victim and what they have gone through.
6. I'm sorry you apologies: This one is pretty bad with politicians. There was a top doctor who lied about the cause of the death of a child, and later was called out on it recently in Canada. She said "It is painful to lose a child without a public debate surrounding the death. I am sorry you felt wronged by my statement". Any apology that shifts the blame from the person who committed the offence to the victim for feeling uncomfortable by that offence is an insincere and harmful apology. Another example is "I'm sorry your feelings got hurt". No apology for hurting their feelings, or any other issue, just that it is wrong the victim felt offended.
7. Any apology that does not bring a conclusion: An apology should consist of several parts. An acceptance of what was done wrong, a promise to not do it again or be more considerate of the victim's position, an asking for forgiveness, and a path forward or a clean separation with the person wronged. Any apology that only ends in self-pity or an "it's ok" does not create growth, it is a stagnant apology. While there is nothing inherently wrong with how it is said, the lack of a path forward creates an expectation that it will continue. Here is an example with, and without, to show how much a solution can do to fix things. "Hey man, I'm sorry I accidentally broke your window playing soccer the other day. That's on me." Compare that with this: "Hey man, I'm sorry I accidentally broke your window playing soccer the other day. I'll be more careful next time, and we can move the location to the park if your parents don't want to risk it happening again. I've got a slight raise in my allowance recently, and I can chip in to fix the window. Again, I am sorry it happened. Will you forgive me?"
8. Apologies that provide closure, but promises are not kept: This ties in with 7, but it is self explanatory. You are less likely to forgive someone who goes back on their word, or be forgiven if you break their trust again.

Many apologies are flawed in one way or another. Whether it be insincerity, a projection of guilt onto the person wronged, or a lack of a path forward and closure to the disagreement or dispute, there are many ways an apology could go sideways. There are probably a few other ways I have not mentioned here, but that is what I could think of right now. If you identified some of these traits as being a common pattern in your apologies, I suggest you train yourself on how to make a proper apology and apologize (lol) to people who you've manipulated or failed to maintain the respect of because of previous unhealthy apologies.

Cheers
