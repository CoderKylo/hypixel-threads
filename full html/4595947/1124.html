....Well, I got sick. That was fun. But it also meant that I couldn't play minecraft because even moving my arm or fingers would instantly give me a headache and give me some REALLY bad vertigo. And that meant I couldn't give y'all a nice update. Until I came up with a genius idea. I would watch youtube videos. Very dramatic, I know.
I watched a lot of intel edits, stimpy, oZook x Parccer, and really analyzed their gameplay. (Nothing better to do i mean) And they all promoted one thing in particular, which was S-tapping. They were able to get crazy combos with the power of the S-tap. So I wanted to find out how they did it, and when to do it.
See, I already know about S-tapping. You use it to create space. But just like all my other combo methods(w-tapping, blockhitting), I still don't really know how to time it. There has to be a certain rhythm to it. For example, Stimpy doing boxing. When he straightlines, he seems to be blockhitting every few hits as to create space BUT not send his opponent out of his reach. His w-taps seem even more rare. Could the rhythm be 2 blockhits per second and 1 w-tap per second? What about the circle strafe then? And where does s-tapping really play into this?
So far, by my own thinking, I think s-tapping is more of a correctional move than a comboing move(w-tapping, blockhitting). It's sort of like using a sidearm in Battlefield than using your primary. It's there to correct distance, not to really start comboing people. I might be wrong on this one.
