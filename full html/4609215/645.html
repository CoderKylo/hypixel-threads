Schrödinger’s Cat and Quantum Immortality​

The Cat Behind it All:

Two years after Schrödinger had won the Nobel Prize, he had created one of the most famous thought experiments known as the Schrödinger’s Cat paradox.

Schrödinger’s Cat is one of the most well-known paradox in quantum mechanics. To learn more about quantum mechanics, it is suggested below this thread (probably).

The paradox introduces the insane idea of a simultaneously alive and dead cat. “How is this possible?”, you may be asking? Well, quantum mechanics is already so strange on its own.

So, basically, the Schrödinger Cat experiment is the event of how a cat can be both dead or alive without us knowing. This means there are two possible timelines: one in the event of the cat being alive and the other of the cat being dead. The result of what happens, dead or alive, remains unknown at all times.

In the experiment, there is some poison thing that has a 50% of releasing the poison. This means there is a 50% chance of the cat being alive/dead. Until we observe it, the cat is both dead and alive. We can’t know if it’s dead since we haven’t observed it dead. Back to what I was saying above, there are 2 different timelines created and this will tie into the next topic below.

Quantum Immortality:

Quick, imagine that you can’t think.

”But how? That isn’t possible, Wallress.”

That is a fact. It is not possible. You can’t imagine a loss of consciousness. But what if I said you were immortal until you die? That sounds pretty stupid, don’t you think? It’s like saying, “You haven’t lost until you lose.” Like, yeah, obviously. But that isn’t the case.

Pretend that you are in this capsule. There is physically no way to escape it. Although, there is a button connected to a mechanism that has a 50% chance of killing you and a 50% of not doing anything.

You are Schrödinger’s Cat.

So, you press the button. Nothing happens. You press it again. Nothing happens. You press it 28 more times. Nothing happens.

Right now, there is a 0.0000000931% chance of you being alive.

Anyhow, each time you pressed that button, 2 new timelines were created: 1 where you are alive and the other where you are dead. You will never end up in the timeline where you are dead because that is not possible.

This is where the name “Quantum Immortality” comes in.

So basically, you are immortal, until death. However, this theory is pretty controversial since we can’t prove it. But because we can’t prove it, it could be true for all we know.
